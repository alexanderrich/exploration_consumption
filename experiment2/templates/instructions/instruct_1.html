Instructions

In this experiment, we are looking at how people make decisions to trade off between fun and boring activities. You will perform two types of tasks—a boring and unpleasant slider task and a fun video task. Throughout the experiment you will make choices that can allow you to spend less time on the slider task and more time on the video task.

The tasks are split into 30-second work periods. In the slider task, five sliders will appear on the screen. Your job is to move each one to "50" using the mouse within the allotted 30 seconds. When the slider is released at 50 it will turn green, showing that it has been completed.

At the beginning of each slider task, only the first slider will be enabled and the other four will be grayed out. As the period progresses, the other sliders will be enabled at 5 second intervals.

An intermittent, annoying static noise will also play throughout the slider task.

The time left in the current work period, number of work periods remaining in the experiment, and percentage of sliders missed over the course of the experiment will be displayed at the top of the screen.
