Instructions—decision-making task

In each room you will play a simple card game. On the left you will see a face-up "best-yet" card, and on the right you will see a deck of face-down cards. You can click the best-yet card to take its value, or click the deck to reveal a new card and take its value. After choosing, click the "click for outcome" button to see the outcome of your choice.

One third (33%) of the cards in the deck have a value of 0. The other two thirds (67%) have values evenly spread from 12 to 36, in multiples of three (in other words, 12, 15, ... 33, 36). The decks in each room are well-shuffled and are large enough that they won't run out of any card values. At the beginning of the game, the dealer in each room will choose a card with a value of 12, 15, or 18 as the starting best-yet card.

If you choose the best-yet card, or you choose a card from the deck but its value is lower than the best-yet card, then the best-yet card will remain the same. But if you pick a card from the deck and it's higher than the current best-yet card, it will become the new best-yet card for your next visit to the room. This means that choosing a card from the deck might sometimes lead to low values, but it can also help you in the long run by improving the best-yet card.
