Instructions

This experiment will take about 60 minutes to complete. To participate in this experiment, please be sure that:

  • You can comfortably adjust computer sliders with a mouse for several minutes.
  • Your internet connection is fast enought to stream Youtube videos.
  • You have time to complete the HIT with minimal interruption.
If you do not meet these conditions, please return the HIT.

In this experiment, we are looking at how people make decisions to trade off between fun and boring activities. At the beginning of the experiment, you will be given a boring and monotonous task to perform. However, throughout the experiment you will make choices that can allow you to "get out of" the boring task and watch a Youtube video instead.

Your task is simply to adjust sliders to "50". Your work will be split into 30-second work periods. In each period, five sliders will appear on the screen. Your job is to move each one to "50" using the mouse within the allotted 30 seconds. When the slider is released at 50 it will turn green, showing that it has been completed.

At the beginning of each work period, only the first slider will be enabled and the other four will be grayed out. As the period progresses, the other sliders will be enabled at 5 second intervals.

The time left in the current work period, number of work periods remaining in the experiment, and percentage of sliders missed over the course of the experiment will be displayed at the top of the screen.
