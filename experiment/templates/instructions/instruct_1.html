Instructions

This experiment will take about 60 minutes to complete. If you do not have time to complete this HIT with minimal interruption, or could not comfortably play a mouse-based video game for that amount of time, please return the HIT.

In this experiment, you will be acting as a worker who must perform a simple but monotonous task. However, throughout the experiment you will make choices that can reduce your time spent on the task and allow you to play a game instead.

Your task is to adjust sliders to "50". Your work will be split into 30-second work periods. In each work period, five sliders will appear on the screen. Your job is simply to move each one to "50" using the mouse within the allotted 30 seconds. When the slider is released at 50 it will turn green, showing that it has been successfully completed.

At the beginning of each work period, only the first slider will be enabled and the other four will be grayed out. As the period progresses, the other sliders will be enabled at 5 second intervals. This means that in each period you must start with the first slider and work your way down. When the final slider is enabled, you will have a total of ten seconds remaining to complete the sliders.

The time left in the current work period, number of work periods remaining in the experiment, and percentage of sliders missed over the experiment will be displayed at the top of the screen.
