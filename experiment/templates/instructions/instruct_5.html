Instructions

If you choose "run new spinner", a random new spinner will be created. The gold wedges on the new spinner can cover anywhere from 0% to 100% of its area. The wedge area on each new spinner is totally random and doesn't depend on the value of past spinners.

After the new spinner is created, the machine will run the new spinner. If the new spinner is worse than the current spinner, the machine will then simply "forget" the new spinner and will remain on its current spinner for future uses. But if the new spinner is better than the current spinner, it will be saved and will become the current spinner the next time you use the machine. This means that while running a new spinner can be risky, it can also allow you to improve the machines so they are more likely to work in the future.

For example, if the current-spinner gold wedges cover half of the spinner, then choosing either the current spinner or a new spinner will give you a 50% chance of the machine working. But, because a good new spinner will be saved, choosing to try a new spinner could increase the chance of the machine working in future uses as well.
